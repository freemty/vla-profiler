EPDA on the Roofline — prediction & exp08a pilot falsification

“EPDA” = the four serving-pipeline phases of a Vision-Language-Action stack. Extends DistServe’s PD (LLM only) and recent EPD Disaggregation (VLM) to include the diffusion-based action phase (A) — a bottleneck class absent from LLM serving.

Action
Diffusion/flow DiT runs k denoise steps for one action chunk
NitroGen DiT, Pi-Zero Expert, Fast-WAM
Lineage · DistServe (OSDI'24) disaggregated P + D for LLM serving. EPD Disaggregation (ICML'25) added E for VLMs. exp08 (this work) adds A — the diffusion action phase, which has no counterpart in LLM serving. Our pilot below tests whether the roofline predictions for pair-wise co-location between these phases hold.
Measured on RTX 5880 Ada (231.6 TFLOPS BF16, 960 GB/s, ridge @ 241 FLOPS/byte) · data from exp01a / exp03a / exp04a / exp06a / exp07a

Roofline positions — where the phases live

The gap below each ceiling tells the story. D rides the BW ceiling (well-tuned decode). P sits 6× below tensor-core peak — L=276 is too thin for GEMM saturation. A is two orders of magnitude below its nominal BW ceiling: kernel dispatch, not compute or bandwidth, is the bottleneck.

Falsifiable prediction for exp08a: D+A co-location will show the largest mutual slowdown (both contend on the memory controller: D for bandwidth, A for per-kernel launch serialization). If this matrix is confirmed, EPDA disaggregation has a structural reason — not just an empirical one.

Roofline under-predicted 3 of 4 scenarios. Only DA·A matches its range. LLM-side phases (P, D) inflated 3.15× and 3.52×, i.e. 2-28× the predicted magnitude. The ordering D>P is preserved but the absolute gap roofline expected (low vs high) collapsed.
LLM side inflates harder than A side. Asymmetric: 3.15-3.52× (LLM) vs 1.37-1.58× (DiT) under identical co-location. Hypothesis: LLM attention kernels are larger and less-predictable in shape; they are preempted by DiT’s smaller regular kernels on the same SM. Contention source is likely kernel launch queue / SM scheduler, not FLOPs/BW.
Implication: Roofline alone is not enough to rank EPDA co-location pairs. But every measured pair shows material inflation (≥1.37×), so EPDA disaggregation motivation is strengthened, not weakened. Next: extend to full 6×6 matrix and propose a GPU-kernel-level contention model that closes the roofline gap.

What's still open in 2026-Q2: (1) Pair-wise co-location interference quantification — no predictive model for which stages can safely share a GPU. (2) Robotics/VLA closed-loop SLO — vLLM-Omni examples are all chat/T2I/T2V, no action-chunk semantics. (3) GPU kernel-level contention model — our pilot shows roofline underestimates by 2-28×.
What exp08 is no longer: a new EPDA serving framework. vLLM-Omni already did that. Instead, exp08 becomes a benchmark + mechanism study on top of vLLM-Omni / SGLang Diffusion, with robotics SLO as the unique scene and the kernel-contention model as the unique theoretical contribution.
